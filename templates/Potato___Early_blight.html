Cause:

• Early blight of potato is caused by the fungal pathogen Alternaria solani. The disease affects leaves, stems and tubers and can reduce yield, tuber size, storability of tubers, quality of fresh-market and processing tubers and marketability of the crop.

Symptoms:

• The early blight is first observed on the plants as small, black lesions mostly on the older foliage.
• Spots enlarge, and by the time they are one-fourth inch in diameter or larger, concentric rings in a bull's eye pattern can be seen in the center of the diseased area. Tissue surrounding the spots may turn yellow.

Treatment:

• Treatment of early blight includes prevention by planting potato varieties that are resistant to the disease.
•Late maturing are more resistant than early maturing varieties.
• Avoid overhead irrigation and allow for sufficient aeration between plants to allow the foliage to dry as quickly as possible.
