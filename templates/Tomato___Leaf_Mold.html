Cause:

• Tomato leaf mold is caused by a fungal pathogen called Passalora fulva (syn. Cladosporium fulvum).
• It is an ascomycete fungus that lives on living tomato leaves. The fungus produces conidia that infect the lower surfaces of leaves.

Symptoms:

• Symptoms of tomato leaf mold appear usually with foliage, but fruit infection is rare.
• The primary symptom appear on the upper surface of infected leaves as a small spot pale green or yellowish with indefinite margins, and on corresponding area of the lower surface, the fungus begins to sporulate.

Treatment:

• In ongoing field studies, Champ 30 WG and Zonix showed the best control of tomato leaf mold in organic trials that took place in 2017 and 2018.
• Control methods for conventional growers include the Quadris Top product, containing azoxystrobin and difenoconazole or products such as Bravo Weather Stik, containing chlorothalonil.
• An apple-cider and vinegar mix is believed to treat the mold effectively. Corn and garlic spray can also be used to prevent fungi outbreaks before they even occur.
• There is also a milk-based spray that is touted as an all-natural, helpful cure for leaf mold.
