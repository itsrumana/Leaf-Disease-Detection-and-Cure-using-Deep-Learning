Cause:

• Tomato red spider mite (Tetranychus evansi) is a very tiny species of mite.
• Spider mites are so named because some species spin protective silk webs, similar to those made by spiders.

Symptoms:

• The tomato red spider mites are pale-orange to red and feed on the underside of leaves. They cannot be seen easily with the naked eye.
• Feeding damage caused by sucking sap appears as many shiny pale yellow marks on the top of the tomato leaf.
• ventually the leaves turn brown and die or fall off. Severe attack leads to formation of webs on the plant (see photo).
•Red spider mites are difficult but not impossible to control.
